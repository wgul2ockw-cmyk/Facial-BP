How it works

Remote Photoplethysmography (rPPG)

This app detects sub-pixel color changes in your facial skin caused by pulsating blood flow. Each heartbeat pushes blood into dermal capillaries, changing how much green light (542-577nm) your skin absorbs. The camera captures these ~0.1-0.5% intensity variations.

Processing pipeline

  1. Face detection — MediaPipe FaceMesh provides 468 facial landmarks
  2. ROI extraction — Forehead, left cheek, and right cheek regions are isolated
  3. Spatial averaging — RGB channel means computed per ROI per frame
  4. rPPG signal extraction — POS or CHROM algorithm separates pulse from noise
  5. Bandpass filtering — 4th-order Butterworth (0.7-4.0 Hz) isolates cardiac band
  6. Heart rate — FFT peak detection in the 45-210 BPM range
  7. Blood pressure — Waveform feature extraction → regression model
  8. PTT proxy — Cross-correlation lag between forehead and cheek BVP signals

BP features extracted

Crest time, systolic rise slope (dP/dt max), pulse width at 50% amplitude, systolic/diastolic area ratio, reflection index, augmentation index, SDNN, RMSSD, and PTT proxy.
