ABOUT VITALSCAN

How it works

VitalScan uses remote photoplethysmography (rPPG) to estimate heart rate and blood pressure from a 15-second facial video. Your camera detects tiny color changes in your skin caused by blood flow with each heartbeat.

Pipeline

Camera → MediaPipe FaceMesh (468 landmarks) → 3 ROIs (forehead + cheeks) → POS algorithm → Butterworth bandpass filter → BVP waveform → 28 morphological features → GBR + Neural Network ensemble → SBP/DBP prediction

Model

Gradient Boosting: 300 + 300 decision trees (depth 5), trained on 1,638 samples from 275 real hospital patients across two published clinical datasets.

Neural Network: 4-layer MLP (28→64→32→16→2) with ReLU activations, trained on the same data with standardized inputs.

Ensemble: Final prediction = 0.7 × GBR + 0.3 × NN

Training data

PPG-BP Figshare (Liang et al., 219 subjects, ages 20–89) + PPG-based-BP-assessment (Vasquez et al. 2024, 56 subjects, ages 44–65). SBP range 80–176, DBP range 48–109 mmHg.

Accuracy

GBR 5-fold CV: SBP MAE 3.29 mmHg, DBP MAE 2.39 mmHg (below AAMI/ESH 5 mmHg clinical threshold). Real-world camera accuracy is lower due to domain gap — calibration strongly recommended.

Privacy

Everything runs in your browser. No data is ever sent to any server. Camera frames are processed in real-time and never stored.

Disclaimer

This is a research tool, not a medical device. Do not use for clinical decisions. Always consult a healthcare provider for blood pressure management.
