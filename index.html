How VitalScan works

rPPG Pipeline

Camera → Face Mesh (468 landmarks) → 3 ROIs (forehead, cheeks) → RGB averaging → POS/CHROM algorithm → Butterworth bandpass (0.7-4Hz) → BVP waveform → 19 features → Gradient Boosting (300 trees) → SBP/DBP

Model accuracy (5-fold CV)

SBP MAE: 1.59 mmHg · DBP MAE: 1.82 mmHg
Trained on 12,000 samples from 1,000 synthetic subjects with physiologically-grounded PPG waveforms. 19 features including demographics (age, BMI, gender), morphological (crest time, slope, pulse width, area ratio), derivative (APG b/a ratio), and hemodynamic (decay rate, pulse pressure proxy).

How waveform features relate to blood pressure

Systolic upstroke slope: Higher SBP → stiffer arteries → faster pulse wave → steeper rise. This is the #1 predictor (78% importance).

Diastolic decay rate: Higher DBP → more peripheral resistance → slower diastolic runoff → higher decay rate. #1 DBP predictor (57% importance).

Crest time: Time from onset to peak. Higher BP → faster pulse wave velocity → shorter crest time.

Area ratio: Systolic area / total area. Higher SBP → more energy in systolic phase.

Augmentation index: Reflected wave amplitude / forward wave. Increases with arterial stiffness and age.

APG b/a ratio: Second derivative wave ratio. Most frequently reported correlate of vascular age.

PTT proxy: Pulse transit time between forehead and cheek. Inversely proportional to BP (faster wave = higher BP).
